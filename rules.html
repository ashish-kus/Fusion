Rules for Event

Eligibility: The event is open to all college-level students, whether they are studying art, technology, or any other field.
Teams: Participants may compete individually or in teams of up to four people.
Prompt: The theme of the event is "Artificial Emotion." Participants must use AI tools and machine learning algorithms to create emotionally evocative art that explores themes like love, loss, and joy, or investigates the intersection of technology and emotion with AI-generated images.
Submission: Each team or individual must submit their final artwork digitally, along with a short description of their creative process and how they used AI tools in their creation.
Judging: The submissions will be judged based on creativity, technical skill, originality, and how well the final images reflect the given AI prompts. The judges will consist of a panel of experts in art, technology, and AI.
Prizes: The winners of the event will receive prizes, which may include cash awards, art supplies, or technology-related prizes.
Copyright: All artwork submitted must be original creations of the participants. Any copyright infringement will result in disqualification.
Ethics: Participants are expected to use ethical practices when creating their artwork and must not use AI tools or algorithms to create any artwork that is discriminatory, harmful, or offensive in any way.
Collaboration: Participants are encouraged to collaborate and share their knowledge and skills with each other. The event is designed to foster a community of artists and technologists who can learn from each other and push the boundaries of creativity with AI tools.
Code of Conduct: Participants must adhere to a code of conduct that promotes respect, professionalism, and inclusivity throughout the event. Any behavior that violates the code of conduct will not be tolerated and may result in disqualification.
